Present Tanse

Simple Present Tense is a tense that is used to explain regular, routine or habitual events that continue to occur every day. This tense can also be used to show something that is happening at the moment. Simple Present Tense is used to express repeated or permanent events, statements that are always true, and also to give instructions.

Past Tanse

Simple Past Tense is a form of tense in English that is used to express events or actions that occurred in the past and have been completed. In this verb form, the time of the event, such as yesterday, last week, or last year or the time period such as for two months, for a day, or for an hour can be specifically mentioned.

Present Continious Tanse

The present continuous tense, also sometimes called present progressive tense, is used to describe actions that are happening now, at the moment of speaking, or around this time in general.

Time expressions: often used with the present continuous to show when the action is happening: now, right now, at the moment, today, this week, etc
